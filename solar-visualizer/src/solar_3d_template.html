Assumptions & method
  • Panels: 10×7 in (254×178 mm), 5 W nameplate each — the exact spec size in all calculations (drawn 3× size here so you can see them).
  • Angles: each panel is tilted to its individually optimized aim (found by searching 5° tilt × 15° azimuth at its position with measured shading) — the base point is the hinge; this shows the range hinges must reach.
  • Output = physics-only losses, no generic trim: clear-sky irradiance (Meinel, +1.2% altitude) × conservative shading (lit-fraction² with a further ×0.75 series-mismatch penalty whenever the face is partially shaded) × calculated heat derate (cell temp from ambient 17–35°C + irradiance self-heating, −0.4%/°C) × 0.95 dust. Calibrated on Ben's bench: a '5W' Voltaic P105 measures 3.85W panel-side in full sun; ×0.90 charger, ×0.63 daily chain into the battery (his field-cycle ledger). Rated 5W is never the operating point.
  • Nothing is wired together — every light is its own panel+battery+LED island. Click a light for its runtime per night (battery Wh ÷ measured 1.36W full-RGBW draw).
  • Shading is measured, not estimated: 15 ray tests across each panel face against the real woven geometry, every 10 minutes, averaged over the 10 event days.
  • Not included: wiring/charge-controller losses, battery acceptance, shade sails, parked vehicles.
  • Sun: verified against BRC almanac — solar noon 12:57 PDT, altitude ≈56°, noon shadow due north.
