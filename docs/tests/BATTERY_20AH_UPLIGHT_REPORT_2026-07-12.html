The charge (a story in three throttles)

Charge current into the cell

USB hub’s 500 mA BC1.2 cap → wall brick (~820 mA) → CV taper, throttled the whole way by the 0.26 Ω clip leads (the charger regulates 3.6 V board-side, so the cell fills through the resistance). 6.03 Ah net in from a 3.25 V resting start — the cell arrived ~⅔ full.

Verdict & what it means for the uplights

The label is honest and the cell is excellent — 97% delivered, and a knee so tight that essentially everything above the firmware floor is usable. For the 24 uplights + 16 chandelier fixtures (solar-free), one cell supports 6 h/night × 7 nights at ~450 mA average battery draw — showable RGBW brightness — with margin, no mid-week charging. White-die-heavy palettes (~150 mA) make the week trivial; saturated color is what to budget. Requirements: daytime sleep must verifiably engage (ADR 0023 watchdog rule) and night radio duty-cycle budgeted (~40 mA average ≈ 1.7 Ah/week).

Caveats, honestly: n=1 (second sample on hand — qualify it before the ~40-cell order); warm-room data (~80 °F; playa nights are cooler — a few % derate); capacity measured through compensated 0.263 Ω clip leads (R verified against a DMM at the cell caps; drift over 27 h is the main residual uncertainty, biasing the usable-above-floor split slightly, not the total). Production fixtures need a real connection method for these end-cap protrusions — alligator clips survived 27 h only because they were taped down and nobody breathed on them.

Method notes beyond the 07-06 report

  • On-board charging as instrumentation: the cell doesn't fit any charger on hand, so the BQ25628E charged it through the same INA — giving a charge-side coulomb count (6.03 Ah in), a free polarity check, and the input-limit staircase in the chart above.
  • Lead compensation: path R measured live (DMM at cell caps vs board reading at known current): 0.263 Ω. All cell-side numbers use cell_v = board_v + I(t)×0.263 with I logged continuously. Cutoff was set at 2.40 V board-side to land ≈2.5 V cell-side at tail currents.
  • SDK gotcha found: the PowerFeather SDK's MAX17260 driver caps DesignCap at 16,383 mAh (20 mΩ sense assumption) — Board.init(20000) fails silently into retry purgatory. Gauge configured at 16,000; SOC advisory as always, current unaffected.
  • Gauge cold-POR gotcha reconfirmed twice more (post-rattle boots at ~2.4–2.8 V cell) — looks like no-cell, self-heals on precharge. POWERFEATHER_NOTES.
